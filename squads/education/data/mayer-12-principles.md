# Mayer's 12 Multimedia Learning Principles

> Evidence-based principles for designing multimedia instruction (Mayer, 2021).

---

## Overview

**Foundation:** Cognitive Load Theory + Dual Coding Theory + Generative Learning

**Goal:** Reduce extraneous load, manage essential processing, foster generative processing.

**Application:** Every video, slide, diagram, animation in every lesson.

---

## Category 1: Reduce Extraneous Processing (5 principles)

### 1. Coherence Principle

**Definition:** Exclude extraneous material (interesting but irrelevant).

**Do:**
- Include ONLY information directly supporting the learning objective
- Remove decorative graphics, background music, tangential stories
- Simplify diagrams to essential elements

**Don't:**
- Add "interesting facts" unrelated to core concept
- Use decorative images "to make it pretty"
- Include every detail you know about the topic

**Example:**
- ✓ Diagram showing 3 types of cognitive load (simple, labeled)
- ✗ Same diagram + brain clipart + motivational quote + decorative border

---

### 2. Signaling Principle

**Definition:** Highlight essential material (cues, emphasis, organization).

**Do:**
- Use arrows, circles, bolding, color to direct attention
- Provide headings and outlines
- Use verbal cues ("The key point is...")

**Don't:**
- Present uniform wall of text/graphics
- Assume learner will find the important parts
- Use decoration that competes with signals

**Example:**
- ✓ "Notice the THREE types of load" (bolded, numbered list)
- ✗ Paragraph mentioning types in passing without emphasis

---

### 3. Redundancy Principle

**Definition:** Use narration + graphics, NOT narration + graphics + on-screen text (of same narration).

**Do:**
- Narrate while showing relevant graphic
- Use text for labels, key terms only
- Reserve text for non-narrated content

**Don't:**
- Show full transcript on screen while narrating same words
- Duplicate audio in text form
- Force learner to read and listen to identical content

**Example:**
- ✓ Voice explains CLT while diagram appears (labeled "Intrinsic, Extraneous, Germane")
- ✗ Voice explains CLT + full paragraph of same explanation on screen

---

### 4. Spatial Contiguity Principle

**Definition:** Place text near corresponding graphics (not separated).

**Do:**
- Put labels ON or immediately next to diagram parts
- Integrate text and graphics on same screen area
- Minimize eye movement between related elements

**Don't:**
- Put legend at bottom, diagram at top
- Separate text block from relevant image
- Force scanning back-and-forth

**Example:**
- ✓ Arrow points to "Germane Load" on diagram, label right there
- ✗ Diagram on left, list of terms on right (learner must match)

---

### 5. Temporal Contiguity Principle

**Definition:** Present narration and graphics simultaneously (not successively).

**Do:**
- Sync voice with appearance of related visual
- Show and tell at the same moment
- Avoid delays between audio and visual

**Don't:**
- Play entire audio, THEN show all visuals
- Show graphic, pause, then explain later
- Separate explanation from representation in time

**Example:**
- ✓ As voice says "extraneous load," that section of diagram highlights
- ✗ Voice explains all 3 loads, then diagram appears showing all at once

---

## Category 2: Manage Essential Processing (3 principles)

### 6. Segmenting Principle

**Definition:** Break lesson into learner-paced segments (not continuous).

**Do:**
- Divide into 2-5 minute chunks
- Allow learner to control pacing (pause, replay)
- Separate complex process into steps

**Don't:**
- Present 25-minute uninterrupted lecture
- Force continuous playback
- Combine unrelated concepts in one segment

**Example:**
- ✓ Lesson split into 5 segments: Hook, Concept, Explanation, Example, Practice
- ✗ 25-minute video with no breaks or chapters

---

### 7. Pre-training Principle

**Definition:** Introduce key concepts/terms BEFORE main lesson.

**Do:**
- Define vocabulary upfront
- Show system components before explaining process
- Provide advance organizer

**Don't:**
- Jump into complex explanation without setup
- Assume learner knows prerequisite terms
- Introduce new terms mid-explanation

**Example:**
- ✓ "First, let's define 3 key terms: intrinsic, extraneous, germane. Now let's see how they interact..."
- ✗ "CLT involves intrinsic load—which is the inherent difficulty—and extraneous load—poor design—and..." (learner lost)

---

### 8. Modality Principle

**Definition:** Use narration + graphics, NOT text + graphics (for complex material).

**Do:**
- Explain with voice while showing visuals
- Leverage dual channels (visual + auditory)
- Reserve reading for simple, self-paced content

**Don't:**
- Force learner to read text while processing complex diagram
- Overload visual channel with text + graphics
- Use text when narration would work better

**Example:**
- ✓ Animated diagram with voice explaining each step
- ✗ Same diagram with paragraph of text to read while watching animation

---

## Category 3: Foster Generative Processing (4 principles)

### 9. Multimedia Principle

**Definition:** Use words + graphics, NOT words alone.

**Do:**
- Combine verbal explanation with relevant visuals
- Use diagrams, animations, photos to support text/narration
- Illustrate abstract concepts

**Don't:**
- Present text-only explanations of visual processes
- Rely solely on verbal description
- Ignore power of dual coding

**Example:**
- ✓ "Here's how CLT works" + animated diagram showing load types
- ✗ "Imagine three types of cognitive load..." (no visual)

---

### 10. Personalization Principle

**Definition:** Use conversational style, NOT formal style.

**Do:**
- Write in first/second person ("I'll show you," "you will learn")
- Use polite, friendly language
- Speak directly to learner

**Don't:**
- Use third person ("The learner will observe...")
- Write in academic, distant tone
- Sound like a textbook or legal document

**Example:**
- ✓ "Let's explore how YOU can apply this in your course."
- ✗ "The application of these principles will be demonstrated."

---

### 11. Voice Principle

**Definition:** Use human voice, NOT machine voice (for narration).

**Do:**
- Record with natural human narrator
- Use warm, engaging delivery
- Match voice to content (enthusiastic for exciting, calm for complex)

**Don't:**
- Use text-to-speech/robotic voice
- Rely on monotone, unexpressive narration
- Sound like GPS directions

**Example:**
- ✓ Professional narrator with natural inflection, pacing, emotion
- ✗ AI-generated voice reading script monotonously

---

### 12. Image Principle

**Definition:** People don't necessarily learn better from video of instructor's face (focus on content).

**Do:**
- Show instructor when building rapport, credibility
- Focus screen real estate on content visuals
- Use talking head sparingly (intro, transitions, conclusions)

**Don't:**
- Keep instructor's face on screen entire lesson
- Waste space with large webcam box over content
- Prioritize instructor visibility over content clarity

**Example:**
- ✓ Instructor appears for 30-second intro, then content fills screen
- ✗ Split screen: instructor's face on left, content squeezed on right (entire lesson)

---

## Application Checklist (Every Multimedia Element)

- [ ] **Coherence:** No extraneous material included
- [ ] **Signaling:** Essential information highlighted
- [ ] **Redundancy:** NOT narrating while showing same text on screen
- [ ] **Spatial Contiguity:** Text placed near corresponding graphics
- [ ] **Temporal Contiguity:** Narration synced with graphics
- [ ] **Segmenting:** Broken into learner-paced chunks
- [ ] **Pre-training:** Key terms introduced before complex explanation
- [ ] **Modality:** Voice + graphics (not text + graphics) for complex content
- [ ] **Multimedia:** Words AND graphics (not words alone)
- [ ] **Personalization:** Conversational tone (not formal)
- [ ] **Voice:** Human narration (not machine)
- [ ] **Image:** Instructor face used strategically (not constantly)

---

## Quick Decision Guide

| Situation | Applicable Principles |
|-----------|----------------------|
| Designing slide | Coherence, Signaling, Spatial Contiguity, Multimedia |
| Recording narration | Modality, Personalization, Voice, Temporal Contiguity, Redundancy |
| Structuring video | Segmenting, Pre-training, Image |
| Creating animation | Temporal Contiguity, Signaling, Coherence |
| Writing on-screen text | Redundancy, Spatial Contiguity, Modality |
