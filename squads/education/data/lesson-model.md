# Replicable Lesson Model

> The 7-part structure for every lesson in the Education Engineer Squad.

---

## Overview

**Duration:** 15-25 minutes per lesson (optimal adult attention span)

**Foundation:** Rosenshine's 10 Principles + Mayer's 12 Multimedia Principles + Bjork's Desirable Difficulties

**Goal:** Maximize learning transfer while managing cognitive load.

---

## 7-Part Structure

### 1. Hook Initial (2-3 min)

**Purpose:** Capture attention and activate prior knowledge.

**Frameworks:**
- Keller ARCS (Attention dimension)
- Schwartz Awareness Level matching
- Rosenshine Principle #6 (Review prior learning)

**Agents:** @keller-motivator + @schwartz-analyst

**What it looks like:**
- Provocative question, surprising fact, or relatable problem
- Connects to learner's existing mental models
- Promises clear, immediate value

---

### 2. Core Concept (3-5 min)

**Purpose:** Introduce the single main idea clearly and concisely.

**Frameworks:**
- Rosenshine Principle #2 (Present new material in small steps)
- Mayer Signaling Principle (highlight essential information)
- Bloom Level 2 (Understand)

**Agents:** @rosenshine-instructor + @mayer-multimedia

**What it looks like:**
- One core concept per lesson (not 3-5)
- Clear definition with visual support
- Simplified before complexified

---

### 3. Structured Explanation (5-7 min)

**Purpose:** Build understanding through multiple representations.

**Frameworks:**
- Mayer Coherence Principle (exclude extraneous material)
- Mayer Modality Principle (narration + graphics > text + graphics)
- CLT (manage intrinsic load)

**Agents:** @mayer-multimedia + @rosenshine-instructor

**What it looks like:**
- Narrated visual explanation
- Step-by-step breakdown
- Zero fluff, maximum signal

---

### 4. Analogies & Examples (3-5 min)

**Purpose:** Connect abstract to concrete, new to familiar.

**Frameworks:**
- Mayer Personalization Principle (conversational style)
- Rosenshine Principle #4 (Provide models and worked examples)
- Ambrose Principle #1 (Connect to prior knowledge)

**Agents:** @rosenshine-instructor

**What it looks like:**
- 2-3 concrete examples
- At least 1 analogy to familiar domain
- Worked example showing complete process

---

### 5. Guided Application (3-5 min)

**Purpose:** Supervised practice with scaffolding.

**Frameworks:**
- Rosenshine Principle #7 (Guide student practice)
- Bloom Level 3 (Apply)
- Merrill Principle #3 (Demonstration)

**Agents:** @rosenshine-instructor + @cathy-moore-action

**What it looks like:**
- Learner attempts with instructor support
- Immediate feedback on attempt
- Gradual release of responsibility

---

### 6. Independent Exercise (3-5 min)

**Purpose:** Retrieval practice for long-term retention.

**Frameworks:**
- Bjork Retrieval Practice (testing effect)
- Anderson Assessment Alignment (objective → assessment match)
- Rosenshine Principle #8 (Independent practice)

**Agents:** @bjork-memory + @anderson-assessor

**What it looks like:**
- Learner solves problem alone
- No reference to previous explanation
- Self-check or automated feedback

---

### 7. Strategic Recap (1-2 min)

**Purpose:** Consolidate learning and preview next lesson.

**Frameworks:**
- Bjork Spacing Effect (distributed practice)
- Rosenshine Principle #10 (Weekly and monthly review)
- Merrill Principle #5 (Integration)

**Agents:** @bjork-memory + @rosenshine-instructor

**What it looks like:**
- 3 key takeaways (learner generates, not instructor)
- Connection to next lesson
- Spaced review prompt (e.g., "Review this tomorrow")

---

## Cognitive Load Management

| Section | Intrinsic Load | Extraneous Load | Germane Load |
|---------|----------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Hook | Low | Low | Medium (activation) |
| Core Concept | Medium | Minimal | High (schema start) |
| Explanation | High | Minimal | High (schema building) |
| Analogies | Low | Low | Medium (connections) |
| Guided Practice | Medium | Low | High (application) |
| Independent | Medium-High | None | Very High (consolidation) |
| Recap | Low | None | High (integration) |

**Total:** Managed progression from low to high cognitive engagement, with strategic breaks.

---

## Quality Checklist

Every lesson MUST have:
- [ ] Single core concept (not multiple)
- [ ] Hook matches awareness level (Schwartz)
- [ ] All 4 ARCS dimensions present
- [ ] At least 1 worked example
- [ ] Guided practice before independent
- [ ] Retrieval practice (not just recognition)
- [ ] Visual support (Mayer multimedia principles)
- [ ] 15-25 min total duration
- [ ] Clear connection to learning objective (Bloom)
- [ ] Accessibility compliant (WCAG 2.1 AA)
