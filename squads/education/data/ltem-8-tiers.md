# LTEM — Levels of Teaching Engagement Model (8 Tiers)

> Piotrowski's framework for measuring instructional engagement depth (Piotrowski, 2010).

---

## Overview

**Purpose:** Quantify engagement quality on an 8-point scale.

**Foundation:** Bloom's Taxonomy + Constructivism + Active Learning research

**Application:** Validate that lessons achieve Tier 5+ engagement (not passive consumption).

**Rule:** Tier 1-3 = passive/weak. Tier 4-6 = active/good. Tier 7-8 = deep/excellent.

---

## The 8 Tiers

### Tier 1: Receive

**Definition:** Student passively receives information (listening, watching).

**Activities:**
- Watching lecture video (no interaction)
- Listening to podcast
- Reading text without annotation

**Bloom Level:** Remember (passive)

**Verdict:** **INSUFFICIENT** for learning. Maximum 10% of lesson time.

**Example:** Watching 10-minute lecture on CLT with no pauses or questions.

---

### Tier 2: Respond

**Definition:** Student responds to simple prompts (yes/no, multiple choice).

**Activities:**
- Multiple choice quiz (recognition, not recall)
- True/false questions
- Clicking "next" to advance

**Bloom Level:** Remember (recognition)

**Verdict:** **WEAK**. Better than Tier 1, but still shallow. Limit to formative checks.

**Example:** "Which of these is a type of cognitive load? A) Intrinsic B) Extraneous C) Germane D) All of the above"

---

### Tier 3: React

**Definition:** Student reacts emotionally or offers opinion (not applying knowledge).

**Activities:**
- "What do you think about this?"
- Sharing feelings or preferences
- Voting/polling on opinions

**Bloom Level:** Remember/Understand (affective, not cognitive)

**Verdict:** **WEAK**. Engagement without rigor. Use sparingly (hooks, motivation).

**Example:** "How do you feel about the idea that most courses overload learners?"

---

### Tier 4: Relate

**Definition:** Student connects new information to prior knowledge or experience.

**Activities:**
- "Describe a time you experienced cognitive overload"
- Compare new concept to familiar concept
- Identify examples from own life

**Bloom Level:** Understand

**Verdict:** **ACCEPTABLE**. Minimum tier for learning. First level of active processing.

**Example:** "Describe a course you took that violated Mayer's Coherence Principle. What extraneous material was included?"

---

### Tier 5: Reflect

**Definition:** Student thinks critically about material, evaluates, analyzes.

**Activities:**
- Analyze why a principle works
- Evaluate quality of example
- Compare/contrast two approaches
- Explain reasoning

**Bloom Level:** Analyze/Evaluate

**Verdict:** **GOOD**. Strong cognitive engagement. Target tier for most lessons.

**Example:** "Why does the Modality Principle reduce cognitive load? Explain using dual coding theory."

---

### Tier 6: Recreate

**Definition:** Student applies knowledge to solve problems or create solutions.

**Activities:**
- Design a lesson using principles learned
- Apply framework to new context
- Solve authentic problem
- Produce work following guidelines

**Bloom Level:** Apply/Create

**Verdict:** **GOOD**. Deep engagement. Performance-based. Ideal for practical courses.

**Example:** "Design a 3-minute hook for a course on personal finance using the ARCS model."

---

### Tier 7: Refine

**Definition:** Student iterates on work based on feedback, improves, debugs.

**Activities:**
- Revise lesson after critique
- A/B test two versions and optimize
- Debug flawed design
- Improve based on rubric

**Bloom Level:** Evaluate/Create

**Verdict:** **EXCELLENT**. Metacognitive engagement. Develops expert judgment.

**Example:** "Your hook scored 6/10 on the ARCS rubric. Revise to increase Relevance and Confidence dimensions."

---

### Tier 8: Relate Outward

**Definition:** Student teaches others, mentors, contributes to community of practice.

**Activities:**
- Peer teaching
- Creating tutorials for others
- Contributing to knowledge base
- Mentoring juniors

**Bloom Level:** Create (with metacognitive + social dimension)

**Verdict:** **EXCELLENT**. Highest engagement. Deepest learning (protégé effect).

**Example:** "Create a 5-minute video teaching the 7-part lesson model to a peer. Include one worked example."

## Tier Distribution (Recommended)

For a 20-minute lesson:

| Tier | % of Time | Minutes | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|---------|
| 1-2 (Passive) | 10-15% | 2-3 min | Introduce concepts, provide models |
| 3-4 (Low Active) | 15-20% | 3-4 min | Hook, connect to prior knowledge |
| 5-6 (High Active) | 50-60% | 10-12 min | Core learning (analyze, apply, create) |
| 7-8 (Expert) | 10-20% | 2-4 min | Refinement, peer teaching (if time) |

**Rule:** Minimum 50% of lesson must be Tier 5+.

---

## Scoring a Lesson (LTEM Audit)

**Process:**
1. Break lesson into activities (each 1-5 min segment)
2. Assign tier to each activity
3. Calculate weighted average based on time
4. Identify lowest-tier segments for upgrade

**Example Audit:**

| Activity | Duration | Tier | Justification |
|----------|----------|------|---------------|
| Hook (problem scenario) | 2 min | 3 (React) | Opinion-based, emotional |
| Explain CLT (lecture) | 5 min | 1 (Receive) | Passive watching |
| Worked example | 3 min | 2 (Respond) | "Notice this..." (passive observation) |
| Apply to own course | 6 min | 6 (Recreate) | Design activity |
| Peer feedback | 4 min | 7 (Refine) | Iterate based on critique |

**Calculation:** (2×3 + 5×1 + 3×2 + 6×6 + 4×7) / 20 = (6+5+6+36+28) / 20 = 81/20 = **4.05**

**Verdict:** Below target (should be 5+). Upgrade Tier 1 lecture to Tier 5 (add analysis questions).

---

## Upgrade Strategies

| From Tier | To Tier | How to Upgrade |
|-----------|---------|----------------|
| 1 (Receive) | 4 (Relate) | Add reflection prompts during video |
| 1 (Receive) | 5 (Reflect) | Insert analysis questions every 2 min |
| 2 (Respond) | 4 (Relate) | Change multiple choice to short answer with justification |
| 2 (Respond) | 5 (Reflect) | Ask "why is this answer correct?" |
| 3 (React) | 4 (Relate) | Add "connect this to your experience" follow-up |
| 4 (Relate) | 5 (Reflect) | Ask "why does this connection matter?" |
| 5 (Reflect) | 6 (Recreate) | Convert analysis task to application task |
| 6 (Recreate) | 7 (Refine) | Add feedback loop + revision requirement |
| 7 (Refine) | 8 (Relate Outward) | Require teaching/mentoring component |

## Integration with Other Frameworks

**LTEM + Bloom:**
- Tier 1-2 = Remember
- Tier 3-4 = Understand
- Tier 5 = Analyze/Evaluate
- Tier 6 = Apply/Create
- Tier 7-8 = Create + Metacognitive

**LTEM + Rosenshine:**
- Rosenshine #4 (models) = Tier 1-2 initially, but must lead to Tier 5-6 practice
- Rosenshine #5 (guided practice) = Tier 6 (Recreate)
- Rosenshine #9 (independent practice) = Tier 6-7 (Recreate/Refine)

**LTEM + ARCS:**
- Tier 1-2 can maintain Attention but fail Confidence
- Tier 5-6 build Confidence through mastery
- Tier 7-8 deliver Satisfaction through accomplishment

---

## Quality Gate

**Minimum Requirement:** Average LTEM score 5.0+ per lesson.

**Checklist:**
- [ ] At least 50% of lesson time is Tier 5+ activities
- [ ] No more than 15% Tier 1-2 (passive)
- [ ] Every Tier 1 segment (lecture) followed by Tier 5+ activity within 5 min
- [ ] At least one Tier 6+ activity (application or creation) per lesson
- [ ] Tier 7-8 activities present in capstone/final project

**Failure = BLOCK.** Redesign low-tier segments before proceeding.
