# Checklist: Motivation Design Check

**Version:** 1.0
**Agent:** keller-motivator
**When to use:** During lesson review or motivational strategy planning
**Pass threshold:** 7/8 items minimum

---

## Items

- [ ] **Attention strategies present** — At least 1 attention strategy per module | PASS criteria: Perceptual arousal (surprise/novelty) OR inquiry arousal (curiosity/mystery) in each module
- [ ] **Relevance connections explicit** — How content connects to learner goals/values | PASS criteria: Each module has explicit "Why this matters" section with real-world connection
- [ ] **Goal orientation present** — Learning objectives clear and learner-relevant | PASS criteria: Objectives written in learner language, linked to career/life goals
- [ ] **Motive matching** — Content appeals to diverse motivations (achievement, affiliation, power) | PASS criteria: Activities vary to appeal to different learner motivations
- [ ] **Familiarity and relevance** — Examples use learner-familiar contexts | PASS criteria: Examples/scenarios reference learner's world, not abstract concepts
- [ ] **Confidence scaffolding** — Success opportunities with appropriate challenge | PASS criteria: Gradual difficulty increase, success early and often
- [ ] **Expectancy for success** — Clear criteria and growth feedback | PASS criteria: Rubrics provided, feedback emphasizes progress not just correctness
- [ ] **Satisfaction via meaningful feedback** — Feedback is timely, specific, and actionable | PASS criteria: Feedback within 24-48h, references criteria, suggests next steps

## Veto Conditions (auto-FAIL)
- No relevance connection to learner goals
- No attention strategy in opening
- No scaffolding (difficulty jumps too fast)

## Scoring
- **PASS:** 7/8 or more
- **WARN:** 5-6/8 (motivationally weak)
- **FAIL:** 4/8 or fewer
